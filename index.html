The Economic Catalyst of 1929

The stock market crash of 1929 was not the sole cause of the Great Depression, but it did act as an accelerant. By October, investors had begun to lose confidence in the stability of the market. On October 24, known as Black Thursday, a record 12.9 million shares were traded. This era was marked by high unemployment and a sharp decline in the GDP of most nations.
